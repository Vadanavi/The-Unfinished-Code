Who Are You?
Your background decides what rights you can claim, what property you can hold, and how the law lands on your life across five eras of Indian legal history.
I. Gender
WM
Woman
A Hindu woman in this period holds property only as a limited estate. The land in the household is rarely in your name, and the law of succession was drafted for families that did not list you among the heirs.
W +3J +8P +3C +10
Lower wealth than a man because of limited inheritance rights under the Hindu Women's Right to Property Act, 1937. Property held by a Hindu woman reverts to her husband's male relatives on her death.
MN
Man
A Hindu man in this period sits inside the joint family as a coparcener by birth. Ancestral property passes through your line by survivorship, and the existing law of intestacy treats you as the natural heir.
W +10J +3P +10C +2
II. Caste Background
BR
Brahmin
A Brahmin household combines ritual standing with political access. Most of the joint family property the Act is drafted around sits with families of your standing, so reform reads as a direct cost to your settlement.
W +15J −5P +15
OB
OBC / Shudra
An OBC or Shudra household sits in the middle layer of the hierarchy. Parts of the Act speak to your interests, but the customary order still applies inside the village, and you are often pulled into disputes you did not frame.
W +8J +5P +5C +10
DL
Dalit / SC
A Dalit household is structurally excluded from the property base the Act operates on. Generations of land dispossession sit behind you, and equal succession is the furthest provision from where your community stands.
J +15C +15
III. Class & Livelihood
LD
Landlord
A landlord household holds ancestral land through the Mitakshara coparcenary. Revenue records sit in male names, and any amendment to Section 6 reaches into both the family settlement and the tax position.
W +15J −10P +10
UP
Urban Professional
An urban professional draws a salary and holds a flat or savings as self-acquired property. The Act feels academic, until a sister or daughter in the family files a partition suit.
W +5J +10P +8C +5
SF
Small Farmer
A small farmer works the land each season even when the title is in another name. The distance between what the statute promises and what the patwari records is the distance your life is lived in.
W +3J +8P +3C +15
LA
Lawyer / Activist
A lawyer or activist reads bare acts daily, drafts writ petitions, and sits in on standing committee hearings. Personal law reform is both your case file and your political work.
W +5J +15P +5C +5
IV. Where Do You Live?
DB
Delhi / Bombay
A resident of Delhi or Bombay lives where the Bills are drafted and the Cabinet meets. Bar associations and the press let you follow each stage of personal law reform closely.
W +8J +5P +10
SK
Andhra / TN / Kerala
Your state legislature did not wait for Parliament. State amendments to the Act recognised daughters as coparceners well before the central amendment in 2005.
W +5J +12P +5C +5
NR
UP / Bihar / Rajasthan
UP, Bihar and Rajasthan formed the orthodox opposition that defeated the 1951 Bill. State amendments to Section 6 never arrived here, and reform is contested at every revenue office.
W +3J +3P +3C +12
RT
Remote / Tribal Area
Customary law still governs succession here. Section 2(2) of the Act keeps Scheduled Tribes outside the statute, and the reform may not reach your district at all.
J +5C +15
